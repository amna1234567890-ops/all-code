Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an exquisite mausoleum built in the 17th century by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, this architectural masterpiece is a stunning example of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles. The structure is primarily made of white marble, adorned with intricate inlay work featuring semi-precious stones.

Its symmetrical gardens, reflecting pools, and majestic domes create a serene and romantic atmosphere, attracting millions of visitors each year. The Taj Mahal not only symbolizes eternal love but also stands as a testament to the artistic and cultural achievements of the Mughal era.
