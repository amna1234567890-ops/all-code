Pyramids of Giza

Pyramids of Giza parag The Pyramids of Giza, located on the outskirts of Cairo, Egypt, are among the most remarkable and enduring symbols of ancient civilization. Built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom, the most famous of these pyramids is the Great Pyramid of Khufu, also known as Cheops, which is the largest of the trio and originally stood at 146.6 meters (481 feet). Constructed from millions of limestone blocks, the pyramids served as monumental tombs for the pharaohs

ancient Egyptians' architectural prowess and their beliefs in the afterlife. The complex also includes the Pyramid of Khafre, which features a portion of its original casing stone, and the Pyramid of Menkaure. Adjacent to these structures is the Great Sphinx

, a colossal limestone statue with the body of a lion and the head of a pharaoh, symbolizing strength and wisdom. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Pyramids of Giza continue to captivate millions of visitors with their grandeur, mystery, and historical significance, standing as a testament to human ingenuity and the enduring legacy of ancient Egypt.
