The Eiffel Tower, a monumental iron structure towering over Paris, was originally built for the 1889 World's Fair; its intricate lattice design by Gustave Eiffel has since become an enduring symbol of French ingenuity and architectural prowess. Rising 330 meters into the sky, it offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks, drawing millions of visitors each year who come to witness both its grandeur and the sweeping vistas of Paris below. Illuminated by thousands of lights, the tower transforms at night into a dazzling spectacle, perfectly embodying the romantic allure that defines the "City of Light."
